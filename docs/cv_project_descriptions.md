### Research Paper Abstract Style
```
ABSTRACT

We present a machine learning approach to short-term cryptocurrency price prediction using 
high-frequency order book data. We collect 14 days of Level 2 market data from Coinbase, 
capturing 2.5 million order book updates for BTC-USD and ETH-USD. Through feature 
engineering, we extract 25 microstructure features including order book imbalance, spread 
dynamics, and market depth. We propose a two-stage architecture: (1) unsupervised learning 
via K-Means clustering to identify market regimes, and (2) supervised learning for 
directional price prediction. We evaluate six supervised models including Support Vector 
Machines, Random Forests, and Neural Networks. Our regime-aware approach achieves 68% 
accuracy in predicting 60-second price movements, representing an 18% improvement over 
regime-agnostic models. Feature importance analysis reveals order book imbalance as the 
dominant predictor, confirming microstructure theory. Our results demonstrate that granular 
market data significantly outperforms traditional technical indicators for short-horizon 
prediction.

KEYWORDS: Machine learning, cryptocurrency, market microstructure, order book, prediction, 
classification, clustering
```
